# Cheers School Mobile UX Research Report\n\n## 1. Best Learning Apps -- Key UX Patterns to Reference\n\n### Duolingo\n**What makes it great:** The streak system is the single most powerful retention mechanic in consumer apps. Duolingo's streaks increase user commitment by 60%, badges boost completion rates by 30%, and XP leaderboards drive 40% more engagement. The \"Streak Freeze\" feature alone reduced churn by 21%.\n\n**Patterns to copy:**\n- **Daily goal selector during onboarding** (5/10/15/20 min) -- lets users self-select commitment level\n- **Streak counter** prominently displayed on home screen with flame animation\n- **XP earned per lesson** shown immediately with celebratory animation\n- **Skill tree / path** -- linear progression with branching for optional content\n- **Loss aversion nudges** -- \"Don't lose your 7-day streak!\" push notification\n- **Streak Freeze** -- one free pass per week to maintain streak without completing a lesson\n\n**Relevance to Cheers School:** High. Restaurant staff work irregular shifts. A streak system with freeze capability accounts for days off. XP maps directly to section completion and test scores.\n\nSources: [Duolingo Gamification Secrets](https://www.orizon.co/blog/duolingos-gamification-secrets), [Duolingo Streak System Breakdown](https://medium.com/@salamprem49/duolingo-streak-system-detailed-breakdown-design-flow-886f591c953f), [UX and Gamification in Duolingo](https://uxplanet.org/ux-and-gamification-in-duolingo-40d55ee09359), [How to Design Like Duolingo](https://www.uinkits.com/blog-post/how-to-design-like-duolingo-gamification-engagement)\n\n### EdApp (now SC Training)\n**What makes it great:** Purpose-built for frontline workers including hospitality. Mobile-first microlearning with drag-and-drop templates. Content is broken into bite-sized pieces reinforced with gamified questions. Push notifications can be scheduled at shift-appropriate times.\n\n**Patterns to copy:**\n- **Template-driven lesson slides** -- each slide is one learning point (not a scrolling page)\n- **Drag-and-drop interactive elements** within lessons (matching, ordering, categorizing)\n- **Social learning** -- learners can comment on any slide, adding their own perspective\n- **Push notification scheduling** -- deliver lessons at times that suit shift patterns\n- **Gamification layer** -- leaderboards and rewards on top of microlearning\n\n**Relevance to Cheers School:** Very high. The 68 existing guides are already structured with sections and subsections. Each section could become a \"slide\" in a mobile microlearning format.\n\nSources: [SC Training (EdApp)](https://www.edapp.com/), [EdApp LMS Features](https://staging-site.edapp.com/lms-features/), [EdApp Review 2026](https://uteach.io/platforms/edapp)\n\n### Typsy (absorbed Lobster Ink)\n**What makes it great:** Hospitality-specific with 1,500+ bite-sized video lessons, subtitles in 30+ languages. Industry-endorsed certificates that employers recognize. Clean mobile interface with light/dark mode.\n\n**Patterns to copy:**\n- **Short video lessons** (2-5 min) -- the primary content format, not text\n- **Industry-endorsed digital badges** shareable to LinkedIn and employer profiles\n- **Micro-credentials** mapping to user profiles\n- **Light/Dark mode** toggle (your app already has this)\n- **Progress tracking** visible at course and profile level\n\n**Relevance to Cheers School:** Medium-high. Your guides are text-based, but embedding short video clips (knife skills demo, cocktail technique, POS walkthrough) would dramatically increase engagement.\n\nSources: [Typsy Platform Features](https://ehl.typsy.com/features), [Typsy Reviews - Hotel Tech Report](https://hoteltechreport.com/hr-staffing/hospitality-lms/typsy), [Top 7 Hospitality Training Platforms 2025](https://www.5mins.ai/resources/blog/top-7-hospitality-training-platforms-in-2025)\n\n### Coursera / Udemy Mobile\n**What makes it great:** Course card design with thumbnail, progress bar, and estimated time. Certificate display with shareable links. Progress dashboard showing all enrolled courses at a glance.\n\n**Patterns to copy:**\n- **Course card anatomy:** thumbnail image + title + progress bar + \"X min left\" + category badge\n- **\"Continue Learning\" hero card** at top of home screen -- the single most important course\n- **Certificate with verification URL** -- scannable and shareable\n- **Download for offline** button on each lesson\n\nSources: [eLearning UI/UX Design Guide 2025](https://viartisan.com/2025/05/27/elearning-ui-ux-design/), [Best eLearning Interface Design Examples](https://www.eleken.co/blog-posts/elearning-interface-design-examples)\n\n### Toast Training\n**What makes it great:** Integrated into the restaurant ecosystem. Paperless onboarding from phone. Compliance monitoring built into HR workflow. Payroll integration means training completion can trigger pay rate changes.\n\n**Patterns to copy:**\n- **Paperless first-day onboarding** entirely from phone\n- **Compliance monitoring** with automated alerts\n- **Training tied to pay progression** -- complete certification, unlock pay increase\n- **HR advisor integration** for compliance questions\n\nSources: [Toast Restaurant Employee Tools](https://pos.toasttab.com/solutions/restaurant-employee-tools), [Toast Review 2025](https://www.businessnewsdaily.com/16056-toast.html/)\n\n---\n\n## 2.
